Download Estuary Data Mapper

You can download the EDM application for Mac, Windows, and Linux systems.

For help with downloading or program specifics, contact edm@epa.gov.

Researchers need current and historic data on estuary conditions to make informed decisions on protecting and preserving these unique environments. With around 2,000 estuaries along the five US coastal regions (Atlantic, Gulf of Mexico, Pacific, Alaska, and Hawaii), scientists, in many agencies and organizations have collected a lot of data. It is a challenge to pull all that information together to see the big picture. EPA’s Estuary Data Mapper (EDM) can help do just that.  The application provides a fast, easy way for researchers to zoom into a specific estuary of interest and find current, available data, from many sources, for that system.

The Estuary Data Mapper provides:
  • Inputs to a Geographic Information System-based data model for estuaries and their watersheds.
  • Data for tools and model outputs to assess, visualize, diagnose, predict, prioritize, and manage condition of estuaries and coastal watersheds.
  • A wealth of environmental data such as tidal, hydrologic, weather, water quality, and sediment quality.
  • Ground, satellite, air, and water data from EPA, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), and the US Geological Survey’s National Water Information System (NWIS). Exit

EDM was designed to support the work of environmental scientists (aquatic biologists and chemists), water ecosystem managers, non-governmental organizations and citizen groups focused on water ecosystems, by providing a wide variety of data, and visualization capabilities, that they need.

EDM also includes information about coastal rivers, tributaries, and watersheds. It gives users the ability to display background information, such as cities and roads, to help them explore areas of interest and learn more about the context. This data will help researchers gauge the status of estuary environments, and possible threats—facilitating the use of decision-support tools to help them visualize the effects of potential management actions. For example, having access to nitrogen loading sources and predictive models of seagrass habitat can help communities and watershed organizations find ways to reduce nitrogen loads.

EDM has released a version with expanded data sources on atmospheric deposition, nonpoint and point nitrogen sources and loads to estuaries and their associated watersheds. EPA researchers will continue to incorporate new data resources and update the mapper to help protect these vital, productive ecosystems.
